Forecasting Stock Market Using Machine Learning Approach Encoder-Decoder ConvLSTM

The stock market prediction is a hot topic these days, and predicting the price of a stock is both difficult and important due to the numerous variables at play. There were numerous Machine Learning models submitted for Stock Market Prediction, but Hybrid Models were successful in making accurate predictions. The goal of this study is to create a hybrid Deep Learning model (Encoder-Decoder ConvLSTM) to anticipate stock market prices. We employed historical stock price data S and P500 (Daily Prices) from Yahoo’s financial website and six months dataset of State Bank of Pakistan (Hourly values). Different prediction models have been tested for the S and P500 dataset which is publicly available and after finding out that the proposed model performed well it has been applied to the SBP dataset as well. The effectiveness of the proposed model has been calculated based on the following performance metrics, root means square error (RMSE), mean absolute error (MAE), mean square error (MSE), and mean absolute percentage error (MAPE). When compared to other comparable studies, the experimental findings indicate that the proposed model has the best performance metrics values. As a result, we can infer that our model is appropriate for accurate stock market time series prediction.

Technologies Used:

  • Deep Learning (ConvLSTM)
  • Python (TensorFlow, Keras)
  • Data Analysis (Pandas, NumPy)
  • Time-Series Forecasting
  • Stock Market Data Analysis
  • Model Evaluation Metrics (RMSE, MAE, MSE, MAPE)

Skills Developed:

  • Time-Series Forecasting
  • Stock Market Analysis
  • Model Optimization and Tuning
  • Data Preprocessing and Visualization
  • Model Evaluation and Performance Metrics

Major Flaws and Areas of Improvement:

  • Model performance is heavily dependent on the quality and size of the dataset used for training.
  • ConvLSTM may not be suitable for all types of financial data, as stock markets can be influenced by many non-quantitative factors.
  • The model can be improved by integrating more advanced techniques like reinforcement learning or considering external factors like news and social media sentiment.

Future Improvements:

  • Implementing more robust models such as Reinforcement Learning for dynamic stock market prediction.
  • Incorporating real-time data for continuous learning and adaptation of the model.
  • Exploring more granular data points (e.g., news sentiment analysis, geopolitical events) to enhance model predictions.

Time-Series Prediction of Cryptocurrency Market using Machine Learning Techniques

In the market of cryptocurrency, Bitcoins are the first currency which has gained significant importance. To predict the market price and stability of Bitcoin in the crypto-market, a machine learning-based time-series analysis has been applied. Time-series analysis can predict the future ups and downs in the price of Bitcoin. For this purpose, we have used ARIMA, FBProphet, and XGBoost for time series analysis as machine learning techniques. The parameters on the basis of which we have evaluated these models are Root Mean Square Error (RMSE), Mean Absolute Error (MAE), and R². After conducting experiments on these three techniques, ARIMA was considered the best model for forecasting Bitcoin prices in the crypto-market with RMSE score of 322.4 and MAE score of 227.3. This research can be helpful for investors in the crypto-market.

Multilevel Feedback Queue: Efficient Scheduling and Implementation by Using Dynamic Quantum

A multitasking operating system allows many programs to run at the same time. Efficient CPU scheduling is necessary to handle these services. CPU scheduling algorithms come in various forms, each with advantages and disadvantages. In this project, we implemented a Multi-Level Feedback Queue (MLFQ) algorithm with a static time quantum for the first queue and a dynamic time quantum for the second queue. The Round Robin (RR) algorithm was used in the first queue for processes with short burst times. Processes with longer burst times were moved to the second queue, where we calculated the average burst time of all remaining processes and set that as the quantum for the second queue, applying the Shortest Job First (SJF) algorithm. This technique helped prevent starvation by ensuring that processes with long burst times still received CPU time, with 70% of processes in the second queue successfully getting CPU time. The approach helped maintain fairness and efficiency in multitasking environments.

Technologies Used:

  • Operating Systems Concepts
  • Scheduling Algorithms (Round Robin, Shortest Job First)
  • Dynamic Time Quantum Implementation

Skills Developed:

Major Flaws and Areas of Improvement:

  • Static time quantum for the first queue can sometimes lead to inefficient use of CPU time for certain processes.
  • Dynamic time quantum might require additional overhead in calculating and updating burst times in real-time.

Future Improvements:

  • Refine dynamic time quantum calculation to make it more adaptive to varying workload conditions.
  • Experiment with other scheduling algorithms to compare performance and efficiency across different systems.
  • Integrate priority scheduling to give higher priority to important processes while maintaining fairness for lower-priority tasks.

Optimizing Sales Forecasting with Time-Series Analysis

Sales forecasting is crucial for businesses to optimize inventory, improve cash flow, and better plan for growth. In this project, we applied time-series analysis to predict sales for a retail business based on historical sales data. Various time-series forecasting models, including ARIMA, Exponential Smoothing, and Facebook Prophet, were utilized to analyze past sales trends and make accurate predictions for future sales. The goal was to provide businesses with reliable forecasting techniques to optimize stock levels, minimize costs, and improve operational efficiency. After evaluating multiple models using performance metrics such as Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), the ARIMA model was found to yield the most accurate and reliable results for sales prediction. This approach provides valuable insights to businesses, enabling them to make data-driven decisions and adapt to future trends.

  • Python (Pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn)

  • Model Evaluation Metrics (MAPE, RMSE)

Skills Developed:

Major Flaws and Areas of Improvement:

Future Improvements:

Exploring Sentiment Analysis in Customer Feedback

Understanding customer sentiment is essential for businesses aiming to enhance customer experience, improve products, and increase customer loyalty. In this project, sentiment analysis was applied to customer feedback to gain valuable insights into customer satisfaction. By utilizing natural language processing (NLP) techniques, we analyzed customer reviews, social media comments, and survey responses to classify feedback as positive, negative, or neutral. The goal was to enable businesses to automatically assess customer satisfaction and respond more effectively to their needs. Various machine learning models, including Naive Bayes, Support Vector Machines (SVM), and deep learning models, were evaluated for their ability to accurately predict sentiment. After comparing the results using performance metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score, the deep learning-based models outperformed the traditional machine learning models, showcasing their effectiveness in extracting nuanced sentiments from complex, unstructured data.

Technologies Used:

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  • Machine Learning Models (Naive Bayes, SVM)
  • Deep Learning (Recurrent Neural Networks, LSTM)
  • Python (Pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, Keras)
  • Text Preprocessing (Tokenization, Lemmatization, Stopword Removal)

Skills Developed:

  • Text Preprocessing and Feature Engineering
  • Building and Evaluating Machine Learning Models
  • Sentiment Analysis for Business Insights
  • Deep Learning for NLP
  • Model Optimization and Performance Evaluation

Major Flaws and Areas of Improvement:

  • Challenges in handling ambiguous or sarcastic language in customer feedback, which may lead to misclassification of sentiment.
  • Data imbalance issues, where some classes (e.g., positive feedback) may dominate, leading to biased model performance.

Future Improvements:

  • Incorporating context-aware models (such as transformers like BERT) to better understand the nuances in customer feedback.
  • Using multi-label classification to capture mixed sentiments in a single feedback.
  • Expanding the dataset to include multilingual feedback for global sentiment analysis.
